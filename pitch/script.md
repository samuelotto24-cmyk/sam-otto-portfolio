## Study Notes

**Total time:** ~5:05

**Where to breathe:**
- After "blank stare" in the problem section — let the silence work
- Before "$3,000" on the traction slide — pause, then say it like a fact, not a brag
- After the Mandi numbers — let judges absorb
- Before the closing line — the pause makes it land

**Lines to nail from memory (don't read these):**
- "Fifteen tools. Not one of them talks to the other."
- "They cannot tell you which dollar came back."
- "Three thousand dollars. Three clients. First eleven days."
- "That's not a projection. That's my bank account."
- "The unit economics work from client one."
- "Every client sells the next one. That's by design."
- "Creators built the audience. bysamotto builds the engine."

**New killer line if it fits naturally:**
- "Every client I serve makes the platform smarter. The service is the R&D."

**If you're running long, cut:**
- Slide 5 (comparison) — say "we check every box, nobody else does" and skip
- Slide 9 (GTM) — say "three-phase go-to-market, happy to walk through it in Q&A"
- The engagement engine description in slide 4 — shorten to "comments become email leads, automatically"

**If you're running short, expand:**
- Tell a specific Mandi story: "She had 64 million likes and was paying for seven different tools, and when I asked which one was driving revenue, she pulled out her phone and showed me... a spreadsheet"
- Add color to engagement engine: "Someone comments BURN on her post, and 30 seconds later they're on a branded page entering their email. She woke up to 51 new leads from one post"
- Mention the platform integrations: "TikTok and YouTube are connected and pulling live data. Instagram is pending one verification from Meta — should be live this month"

**Q&A prep:**
- "What's your moat?" → "I'm embedded with the creators, building the system around real workflows. Every tool on that grid built what they thought creators needed. I'm building what they actually use."
- "Can you scale this?" → "That's what phase 3 is. Right now I'm doing it hands-on because the service IS the R&D. Every client I onboard teaches me what to automate next."
- "Why not just use Shopify/Squarespace?" → "They build websites. I build growth systems. The site is the anchor, but the analytics, the AI, the engagement engine — that's what makes the money trackable."
- "What about competitors building this?" → "Linktree raised $110 million and still can't tell a creator which post drove a sale. The big players are locked into their lane. I built the whole thing because I started from zero with no legacy constraints."
- "How did you build this so fast?" → "I'm a developer. I write the code. My cost basis is my time and about $22 a month in infrastructure. That's why the margins are 85%."
- "Could this work for brands too?" → "Already doing it. I have a DTC beauty brand in my pipeline. The platform adapts — but creators are the beachhead."
