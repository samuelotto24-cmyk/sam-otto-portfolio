## Where the Humor Lives

| Moment | Type | Why it works |
|--------|------|-------------|
| "ran out of fingers" | Observational | Makes 15 tools feel absurd |
| "long division" | Character | Gets a laugh AND reinforces the problem |
| "Spotify and gym membership" | Self-deprecating | Humanizes you after a hard number |
| "I'll let that speak for itself" | Understated | Confident, not cocky |
| "I didn't chase them" | Flex disguised as fact | Shows organic demand |

**Rule: humor in the first half (slides 1–5), conviction in the second half (slides 6–11). After the $3,000 hits the screen, no more jokes. You're a founder now.**

## The Q&A Trap

You deliberately don't explain HOW the engagement engine works in detail. You mention it, the dashboard shows it, but the mechanics are left for Q&A. When a judge asks:

> **"How does the engagement engine actually work?"**

You answer:

> "Great question. A creator sets a keyword — let's say Mandi uses BURN for her workout guide. Someone comments BURN on her post. Within 30 seconds, they get an auto-DM with a link. That link goes to a branded capture page. They enter their email. Now Mandi has a lead, and that person gets the guide. She woke up one morning to 51 new email leads from a single post. No extra tools. No ManyChat subscription. It's built in."

This is your best material, and it plays better as a Q&A answer than crammed into five minutes.

## Other Q&A Prep

- **"What's your moat?"** → "I'm embedded with the creators. I'm not guessing what they need from a product lab — I'm building what they actually use. And because I write the code myself, I can ship in 72 hours what a funded startup takes a quarter to spec."

- **"Can you scale this?"** → "That's what phase 3 is. Right now I'm doing it hands-on because the service IS the R&D. Every client I onboard teaches me what to automate next. That's by design."

- **"Why not Shopify or Squarespace?"** → "They build websites. I build growth systems. The site is the anchor, but the analytics, the AI, the engagement engine — that's what makes every dollar trackable."

- **"What about competitors?"** → "Linktree raised $110 million and still can't tell a creator which post drove a sale. The big players are each locked into their lane. I built the whole picture because I started from zero with no legacy to protect."

- **"How did you build this so fast?"** → "I'm a developer. My cost basis is my time and $22 a month in infrastructure. That's why the margins are 85%. I don't have a team to coordinate — I just ship."

- **"Could this work for brands?"** → "Already exploring it. I have a DTC beauty brand in my pipeline. But creators are the beachhead — that's where the pain is loudest right now."

## Physical Delivery Notes

- **Walk-on:** one full second of silence before you speak
- **"Blank stare" / "long division":** pause BEFORE the punchline, not after. The pause creates the laugh.
- **$3,000:** feet planted, hands still, say it flat. The less you do, the bigger it feels.
- **Closing line:** slow your pace by 50%. Each word is its own beat.
- **Eye contact:** pick three people in different parts of the room. Rotate between them. Don't scan.
- **Don't look at the screen.** You know what's on it. Looking at your own slides breaks authority.
